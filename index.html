What is this ?

A simple method for aproximating π using some random points.

How does it work ?

I stumbled on this method while surfing youtube (source).

What we have here is a circle inscribed in a square. The area of the square is A_sqr = Lenght * Length = 4* Radius * Radius; While the area of the circle is A_circ = π * Radius * Radius. If we divide A_circ/A_sqr = π/4 Or π = 4 * A_circ/A_sqr. The next thing to understand is that when we keep generating random points we start filling the Area !!!

It generates random points (x, y) and counts them (Ntotal = total nr. of points generated). Each point is checked it hits the circle (Ncirc = points inside the circle).

All it's left to do is to calculate π = 4 * A_circ/A_sqr = 4 * Ncirc/Ntotal
